ETA 2824-2 · Introduction

Movement Overview

Complete professional overhaul guide based on official ETA Technical Communication CT 2824-2 FDE 481688 (Version 11, 01.09.2010). Work through each section in order — mark sections complete as you go.

⚠Repair and reconditioning work must only be performed by properly trained personnel. Eye protection is obligatory for all operations on the movement. ETA SA disclaims liability for damage from non-compliance.

Step 01 · Disassembly

Pre-Service Inspection

Document the movement condition and record baseline performance before touching a single screw.

1.1
Run timing machine — record baseline
At 4 Hz (28,800 A/h) record rate, amplitude, beat error and positional variance in at least 4 positions before disassembly. This baseline confirms problems existed and verifies the service corrected them.
TIP — do this first
1.2
Check amplitude — expect 270–310° dial up, fully wound
Below 250° in any position indicates dirty/dry pivots, worn escapement, or a weak mainspring (Pos 771). Note all positions.
1.3
Verify power reserve — approximately 38 hours
Fully wind and run down. Significantly shorter than 38 hours suggests a worn or broken mainspring (Pos 771 — Barrel spring NM).
1.4
Check date change timing (if date model)
With hour hand centred on 12, the date change should occur within 30 seconds of 12 o'clock. Record result.
1.5
Release mainspring tension completely
Hold crown in winding position. Use pegwood to press the click (Pos 425) aside. Allow the crown wheel to spin down under controlled thumb pressure on the crown. Never release suddenly — snapped click springs result.
CRITICAL — do before any disassembly
1.6
Photograph the movement — both sides
Clear photos of movement both sides, noting any damage, worn parts, or unusual settings. Reference during reassembly.
TIP
Step 02 · Disassembly

Case Opening & Hand Removal

2.1
Open case back
Snap or unscrew using the correct tool. Inspect gasket condition — note whether it needs replacing. A new gasket must be fitted before the final water resistance test.
2.2
Remove seconds hand — lift straight up
The seconds hand seats on the 4th wheel arbor (Ø 0.7 mm, cylindrical). Lift straight and even — any rocking can bend or break the 4th wheel pivot. Use hand levers with dressed smooth edges, or a hand press. Place hand on a labelled tray.
Lift perfectly straight — 4th wheel pivot at risk
2.3
Remove minute hand
Lever levers under the minute hand close to the cannon pinion. Lift smoothly and evenly. Minute hand bore: Ø 0.9 +0.6/−0.4 mm, cylindrical.
2.4
Remove hour hand
Hour hand bore: Ø 1.5 +0.4/−0.2 mm, 3% conicity. Lever gently upward without rocking. Place all hands in labelled protective tray.
2.5
Release and remove winding stem (Pos 401)
Pull crown to setting position (first click). Use a fine pin to depress the setting lever (Pos 443/1) through the stem release hole — the stem will pull free. Set stem and crown aside.
2.6
Extract movement from case
Push from the case back side. Never grip the balance or rotor. Use movement holder immediately. Check that the centre tube (Pos 161) stayed in the main plate.
Step 03 · Disassembly

Dial Removal & Keyless Work

3.1
Remove dial — release dial fasteners (Pos 144)
Two set screws on the movement edge (Pos 144 — Dial fastener). Do not bend the dial support feet (Pos 145) — they set the precise dial height above the movement.
3.2
Remove hour wheel (Pos 255/1) and cannon pinion (Pos 242)
Lift hour wheel straight off. Cannon pinion (Pos 242 — with driving wheel) is a friction fit on the centre arbor. Pull straight up. If loose, note for staking during reassembly.
Cannon pinion is friction fit only — do not over-force
3.3
Flip movement — begin keyless work disassembly
Place movement dial-side down in movement holder. All keyless work is now accessible from this side.
3.4
Remove combined setting lever jumper (Pos 445/2) — Screw 5445 (1.2 Ncm)
One screw. The jumper is under slight spring tension — control it as the screw lifts free.
Spring tension — control lift
3.5
Remove setting lever (Pos 443/1), yoke (Pos 435), setting wheel (Pos 450), minute wheel (Pos 260)
Remove in this order. The setting lever is an assembled unit. The yoke (Pos 435 — Kupplungstriebhebel) then lifts free. Setting wheel (Pos 450) and minute wheel (Pos 260) drop out.
3.6
Remove sliding pinion (Pos 407) and winding pinion (Pos 410)
Both should drop out freely now the setting lever has been removed.
3.7
Remove minute train bridge (Pos 462) — Screw 5462 (0.8 Ncm)
One screw (Pos 5462, group c — lowest torque in the movement at 0.8 Ncm). Remove bridge.
Step 04 · Disassembly

Automatic Module Removal

The 2824-2's bidirectional automatic module must be removed before accessing the base movement.

4.1
Remove oscillating weight (Pos 1143/1) — Screw 51497 (1.2 Ncm)
Remove ball bearing screw (Pos 51497). Lift rotor straight off — it rests on the ball bearing (Pos 1497). Never set the rotor face down on the bearing surface.
Never rest rotor on its bearing face
4.2
Remove ball bearing (Pos 1497)
If replacing, use appropriate puller. Inspect bearing for roughness, play, or pitting on the inner/outer races. The 2824-2 ball bearing (Kugellager) is a known service part — replace if any doubt.
4.3
Remove auto lower bridge (Pos 1141) — Screw 51141 (1.2 Ncm)
One screw (Pos 51141). The lower bridge is jewelled — note the reduction wheel upper pivot rides here.
4.4
Remove auto wheels in reverse order
Remove: Auxiliary reversing wheel (Pos 1530), Reversing wheel (Pos 1488 — Var), Ratchet driving wheel (Pos 1482), Reduction wheel (Pos 1481). Keep in labelled tray — these wheels are specific to the 2824-2 winding direction logic.
4.5
Remove automatic framework (Pos 1134) — Screws 51134 ×2 (1.6 Ncm)
Two screws (Pos 51134, group b — 1.6 Ncm). Lift the framework. The base movement is now fully exposed.
Variant note: Reversing wheel (Pos 1488) is marked "Var" — confirm the correct variant via ETA Online Shop (EOS) at eta.ch before ordering replacements.
Step 05 · Disassembly

Balance & Escapement

⚠Always balance first, pallet second. Removing the pallet fork before the balance allows the escape wheel to spin freely and can shatter a balance pivot instantly.
5.1
Remove balance bridge screw (Pos 5121) — 1.2 Ncm
One screw. Loosen but do not remove completely until the bridge has been worked free from its locating pins.
5.2
Lift balance bridge (Pos 121/3) — perfectly parallel to the plate
This is one of the highest-risk operations. The balance bridge must come off completely level — any tipping will snap the upper balance pivot in the shock absorber (Pos 3024). Use both hands if needed. One side should not rise before the other.
Most common cause of broken pivots — keep level
5.3
Remove balance wheel (Pos 721)
Lift by the rim — never by the hairspring or collet. Place immediately in a covered container. The hairspring is adjusted with the ETACHRON system (Pos 303/2, two-piece regulator) — do not disturb the regulator pins.
Never touch the hairspring
5.4
Inspect both shock absorbers (Pos 3024 top, Pos 3025 bottom)
Top shock absorber (Pos 3024 — shouldered, press-in, to balance bridge). Bottom (Pos 3025 — cylindrical, press-in, to main plate). Under 10× magnification: check chaton seating, endstone condition, no cracks. Replace if endstone is chipped or chaton is loose.
10× minimum magnification
5.5
Remove pallet bridge (Pos 125) — Screws 5125 ×2 (1.2 Ncm)
Two screws (Pos 5125, group a). Lift straight up. Pallet bridge is jewelled — note pivot condition.
5.6
Remove pallet fork (Pos 710) using pegwood only
Use pegwood to tilt and lift the pallet fork. Never grip the pallet body with tweezers — the pallet jewels are shellac-bonded on many variants and the body is vulnerable to jaw marks. Do not allow the fork to roll over onto the pallet jewels.
Pegwood only — no tweezers on the pallet body
5.7
Remove stop lever (Pos 9433)
The stop lever (Stopphebel) prevents the train running when the crown is in the setting position. Lift out and place in tray.
5.8
Inspect pallet jewels under 10×+
Check both jewels for: chipping on locking faces, deformation, uneven wear on impulse faces. Any chipping means replace the pallet fork (Pos 710). Note: the locking face is the critical surface — micro-chips cause severe rate errors.
Step 06 · Disassembly

Train Wheels & Barrel

6.1
Remove train wheel bridge (Pos 110) — Screws 5110 ×2 (1.2 Ncm)
Two screws (Pos 5110, group a). Lift slowly and evenly — all three train pivots disengage simultaneously. Any tipping can bend a pivot.
6.2
Remove train wheels: escape wheel (705), second (227), third (210), intermediate (203)
Remove in order, wheel by wheel. Place each on a labelled component tray. Inspect escape wheel teeth for bent tips, damage, or flat spots.
6.3
Remove barrel bridge (Pos 105) — Screws 5105 ×3 (1.2 Ncm)
Three screws (Pos 5105, group a).
6.4
Lift out barrel complete (Pos 180/1)
Check: barrel rotates freely? Any roughness in the barrel bearing indicates wear. Centre tube (Pos 161) stays in the main plate — it is the bearing surface for the cannon pinion.
6.5
Remove winding mechanism — click spring, click, ratchet wheel, crown wheel
Order: Click spring (Pos 430) → Click (Pos 425) → Ratchet wheel (Pos 415, Screw 5415 — 1.6 Ncm) → Crown wheel (Pos 420, Screw 5420 — 1.6 Ncm). Crown wheel screw may be left-hand thread — turn clockwise to loosen on LH variants.
Crown wheel screw — may be LH thread
6.6
Open barrel — inspect mainspring (Pos 771)
Use barrel opener. Remove drum (Pos 185), then the mainspring (Pos 771 — NM, non-magnetic). Check for: kinks, rust spots, fractures near the outer end, loss of curvature (set). A healthy spring coils with consistent curvature throughout. Replace if any doubt — mainspring failure after service is an embarrassment.
6.7
Remove barrel arbor (Pos 195) — inspect pivot
Barrel arbor (Pos 195 — Federwelle) slips from the main plate. Inspect pivot for wear. Check the main plate barrel arbor bore — should be snug with no wobble.
6.8
Remove date mechanism (if present)
Order: Date indicator driving wheel (Pos 2556) → Date indicator (Pos 2557/1) → Date jumper maintaining plate (Pos 2595/1, Screw 52595 — 1.2 Ncm) → Date jumper ×2 (Pos 2576) → Date corrector (Pos 2566) → Double corrector lever (Pos 2570).
Step 07 · Cleaning

Ultrasonic Cleaning

All parts must be thoroughly cleaned before reassembly. Old oil mixed with metal particles becomes an abrasive paste — the primary cause of accelerated wear between services.

DO NOT Ultrasonically Clean
— Balance wheel with hairspring (Pos 721)
— Mainspring (Pos 771) — clean by hand
— Any pallet fork if shellac-bonded jewels are suspected
— Anything with rubber seals or cemented parts

Clean these by hand with pegwood and IPA or petroleum ether.
Cleaning Sequence
1. Pre-clean in petroleum ether (dissolve old grease/oil)
2. Ultrasonic bath — watch cleaning fluid
3. Ultrasonic rinse — clean fluid
4. Dry in heated cabinet 40–50°C
5. Inspect every part under magnification
6. Peg out all jewel holes before reassembly
7.1
Sort parts into cleaning baskets
Bridges separately (jewel damage from contact), wheels together, screws in separate mesh container. Label clearly — the 2824-2 has many similar-looking screws with different torque specs.
7.2
Handle pallet fork carefully — risk of shellac attack
If unsure whether pallet jewels are shellac-bonded, clean by hand with petroleum ether only. IPA dissolves shellac and the jewels will fall out. Peg clean the upper and lower pivot holes.
Shellac risk with IPA
7.3
Peg out all jewel holes — do not skip this
After drying, use sharpened pegwood to clean each jewel hole in all bridges and the main plate. Insert and rotate — never scrape. Change the pegwood tip frequently — it quickly becomes contaminated. Clean endstones of shock settings individually. This step alone can recover 10–20° of amplitude.
Not optional — amplitude depends on this
7.4
Inspect all pivots under 10×+
Check all wheel pivots: bending, scoring, pitting. Balance pivots (Pos 721) are most delicate — a bent balance pivot is cause for balance wheel replacement (Pos 721 is a complete timed and standardised assembly including stud). Escape wheel pivots are also fragile.
7.5
Inspect barrel drum interior
Barrel drum (Pos 185): fine scratches inside from a broken spring are normal and acceptable. Deep gouges are not — they catch the spring and prevent free unwinding. Clean inside with pegwood.
7.6
Clean balance wheel by hand
Hold balance by rim only. Gently wipe pivots with soft pith wood or clean chamois. Blow with a soft dry air bulb (never compressed air — it can disturb the hairspring coils permanently). Check the hairspring: every coil should be flat, evenly spaced, and perfectly concentric when the balance is at rest.
Step 08 · Reassembly

Barrel & Mainspring

8.1
Apply HP-1300 / D5 to inside of barrel drum (Pos 185)
Thin smear on the inner wall using a pegwood stick or small brush. Too much grease loads the mainspring and reduces power reserve. Aim for a thin, even, just-visible film.
LUBE: HP-1300 / D5
8.2
Wind mainspring (Pos 771) with mainspring winder
Always use a winder — never hand-fit a mainspring. Select correct arbor size. Ensure the outer end hook (bridle) engages the barrel cutout correctly before loading. The NM spring should coil smoothly with no kinks. Inner end attaches to the barrel arbor hook.
Always use a mainspring winder
8.3
Close barrel drum with barrel press
Use a barrel press — never finger pressure. The drum lid must seat fully and evenly around the entire circumference. A partially closed barrel causes the lid to drag against the barrel bridge.
8.4
Oil barrel arbor pivots (Pos 195)
Lower pivot: Moebius 9010 — applied to the main plate barrel arbor jewel. Upper pivot: HP-1300/D5 — applied to the barrel bridge jewel hole. Pre-oil the upper now before the barrel goes in, so you do not need to revisit it.
LUBE: 9010 lower · HP-1300 upper
8.5
Fit barrel complete (Pos 180/1) into main plate
Lower the barrel assembly into the main plate. Arbor seats in the main plate hole. The barrel should rotate freely. Check end shake — barrel end shake should be the greatest in the movement (decreasing through the train to the escape wheel).

Pre-oil upper pivots of intermediate (203), second (227), third wheel (210)

9.2
Fit barrel bridge (Pos 105) — Screws 5105 ×3 (1.2 Ncm)
Lower the barrel bridge over the barrel arbor. Three screws (group a). Check barrel rotates freely after tightening.
9.3
Lower train wheel bridge (Pos 110) slowly — all three pivots simultaneously
Lower bridge with all three train wheel pivots entering their jewels at the same time. Any resistance means a pivot is not seated — do NOT press down. Lift, reposition, try again. This is a skill that develops with practice.

9.4
Fit Screws 5110 ×2 (1.2 Ncm) — verify free rotation
Tighten both screws. All train wheels should spin freely under finger pressure with no binding. Check end shake on each wheel — each should have less end shake than the barrel, decreasing through the train.
9.5
Oil all lower pivot jewels via main plate (accessible from below)
Apply 9010 to every lower pivot jewel visible through the main plate: intermediate (Pos 203), second (Pos 227), third (Pos 210), and escape wheel (Pos 705). Use smallest oiler — apply just enough to form a complete oil ring around the jewel. An oil ring that extends past the jewel edge means too much was applied.
LUBE: 9010 — all lower pivot jewels
9.6
Fit escape wheel (Pos 705)
Set the escape wheel pivot into the train bridge jewel. Apply 9010 to the lower escape wheel pivot via the main plate jewel. Also apply 9010 to the upper escape wheel pivot via the train bridge jewel.
LUBE: 9010 — escape wheel both pivots
9.7
Apply HP-1300/D5 to barrel arbor upper pivot (barrel bridge jewel)
Apply a small dot of HP-1300 or D5 to the barrel arbor upper pivot hole in the barrel bridge. This is a higher-load bearing than the train wheel pivots — fine oil is insufficient here.
LUBE: HP-1300 — barrel arbor upper pivot
9.8
Fit stop lever (Pos 9433)
Fit the stop lever (Stopphebel) into its position on the train bridge. It should be free to deflect and spring back. Test with the stem: pulling the crown to setting position should engage the stop lever against the escape wheel (if it is a model with this feature).
Step 10 · Reassembly

Winding Mechanism

Crown wheel screw (Pos 5420) may be left-hand thread — tighten counter-clockwise on LH thread variants. Always check thread direction before applying torque.
10.1
Fit crown wheel (Pos 420) — Screw 5420 (1.6 Ncm)
Lower the crown wheel (Kronrad) into position. Apply correct screw direction. Tighten to 1.6 Ncm. Apply a light film of HP-1300/D5 to the crown wheel teeth.
LUBE: HP-1300 — crown wheel teeth
10.2
Fit ratchet wheel (Pos 415) — Screw 5415 (1.6 Ncm)
Place ratchet wheel (Sperrad). Tighten screw at 1.6 Ncm. Apply HP-1300/D5 to the ratchet teeth. The click must engage cleanly on every tooth.
LUBE: HP-1300 — ratchet teeth
10.3
Fit click (Pos 425) and click spring (Pos 430)
Fit click spring first, then click. The click spring must have sufficient tension to lock the click firmly under winding load. Apply a drop of HP-1300/D5 at the click-to-ratchet contact point.
Verify click spring tension
10.4
Test winding — 8–10 rotations
Wind through 8–10 full crown rotations. Click should engage positively on every tooth. No slipping. The train should not run while winding (stop lever holds it). Good solid winding feedback means the mechanism is correct.
Step 11 · Reassembly

Pallet Fork & Balance Wheel

The most critical assembly sequence. Precision lubrication here defines the movement's performance for the next 5–7 years.

11.1
Oil pallet jewels with 941 or 9415 — impulse faces only
Use a fine oiler to apply a tiny, barely-visible film of Moebius 941 or 9415 to the impulse face of each pallet jewel (entry and exit stones). The impulse face is the angled, sliding surface. The locking face (vertical surface) receives NO oil — oil on the locking face destroys the lock and ruins rate. One application per jewel. If you apply too much, re-clean and start over.
LUBE: 941 / 9415 — impulse face onlyMost critical lube point in the movement
11.2
Fit pallet fork (Pos 710) with pegwood
Lower pallet fork with pegwood guidance — never grip the pallet body with tweezers. Pivot must seat in the main plate jewel. The fork should hang freely and rock left-right when the escape wheel is flicked with pegwood.
11.3
Fit pallet bridge (Pos 125) — Screws 5125 ×2 (1.2 Ncm) — oil upper pivot
Lower bridge over upper pallet pivot. Two screws at 1.2 Ncm. Apply micro-drop of 9010 to the pallet pivot via the jewel in the pallet bridge. Also apply 9010 to the lower pallet pivot jewel in the main plate (accessible from the bottom).
LUBE: 9010 — both pallet pivot jewels
11.4
Test escapement before fitting balance
With a small amount of spring tension (hand-wind 2–3 turns), use pegwood to gently rock the pallet fork from guard pin to guard pin. Watch and listen: the escape wheel should advance exactly one tooth each time, with a crisp, even impulse. Any stuttering, catching, or uneven motion means the escapement needs attention before the balance goes in.
Do this test before fitting the balance
11.5
Oil shock settings (Pos 3024 top, Pos 3025 bottom) — capillary technique
Top (Pos 3024 in balance bridge): With endstone in position, place a single drop of 9010 at the centre of the endstone. Hold a fine oiler just next to the chaton rim — capillary action draws oil into the gap between chaton and endstone. Do not reposition chaton once oiled — all oil moves.
Bottom (Pos 3025 in main plate): Same procedure. Both shock settings must be done before the balance is fitted.
LUBE: 9010 — both shock endstonesOne attempt — repositioning chaton loses the oil
11.6
Verify ETACHRON regulator (Pos 303/2) and stud support (Pos 375) are correctly set
The two-piece regulator (Rücker, Pos 303/2) and stud support ETACHRON (Pos 375) should be in their standard position on the balance bridge. Regulator pins should bracket the hairspring coil without touching it when centred. Regulator corrector (Pos 358) allows fine adjustment without removing the balance.
11.7
Fit balance wheel (Pos 721) — lower pivot into bottom shock jewel
Lower the balance by its rim into the main plate. The lower pivot enters the bottom shock absorber (Pos 3025). Do not touch the hairspring. Set down gently — no bouncing.
Never touch the hairspring
11.8
Lower balance bridge (Pos 121/3) — perfectly level — Screw 5121 (1.2 Ncm)
This mirrors disassembly. The bridge must come down perfectly parallel. The upper balance pivot enters the top shock absorber (Pos 3024) straight — any tilting will snap the pivot. Keep both sides of the bridge at the same height as you lower it. One screw (Pos 5121, group a). Do not overtighten.
Keep bridge perfectly level — #1 cause of broken pivots
11.9
Verify movement is running — check amplitude
Give the movement a small impulse by flicking the crown or the rotor ring. The balance should oscillate freely and maintain rhythm. Listen for even, consistent ticking. If the balance stops immediately: check hairspring is not trapped under the regulator, check shock setting oil has not spread onto the balance rim, check pallet jewel oil is not excessive.

13.1
Date jumper lubrication — 4-step ETA procedure (Pos 2576)
Step 1: Lubricate Pos 2576 with Moebius 9501 or Jismaa 124.
Step 2: Rotate date indicator (Pos 2557/1) approximately 10 teeth using the quick-set corrector.
Step 3: Lubricate Pos 2576 again with 9501/Jismaa 124.
Step 4: Rotate Pos 2557/1 another 10 teeth with quick corrector.
This distributes grease evenly along the entire date jumper path — skipping this leaves unlubricated zones and causes the date to stick after several months.
LUBE: 9501 / Jismaa 124Must follow 4-step ETA procedure exactly
13.2
Fit date indicator (Pos 2557/1)

13.4
Fit minute train bridge (Pos 462) — Screw 5462 (0.8 Ncm)

Verify date change timing
Run movement and advance hands slowly through midnight. The date change (disc snap) should complete quickly and cleanly. Slow or incomplete change indicates insufficient date jumper tension or a worn driving wheel tooth. If change takes more than 5 minutes of the hour-hand arc to complete, investigate before casing.
Step 14 · Reassembly

Keyless Work & Dial Fitting

14.1
Lubricate keyless work contact areas
Apply HP-1300/D5 to: yoke (Pos 435) sliding surface, setting lever (Pos 443/1) pivot pin, sliding pinion (Pos 407) engagement surface. Apply 9010 to cannon pinion (Pos 242) arbor surface and the centre tube (Pos 161) bearing bore.
LUBE: HP-1300 — keyless work · 9010 — cannon pinion arbor
14.2
Insert stem (Pos 401) — verify setting lever engagement
Depress the setting lever and insert the winding stem (Pos 401) until the notch aligns with the setting lever tooth. Release lever — it should spring back and lock the stem securely. The stem should not pull out without pressing the lever again.
14.3
Fit setting lever (Pos 443/1), yoke (Pos 435), combined jumper (Pos 445/2) — Screw 5445 (1.2 Ncm)
Fit in order. Jumper goes on under slight preload. One screw at 1.2 Ncm. Test: crown in winding position — sliding pinion engages winding pinion. Crown pulled to setting position — sliding pinion disengages, setting wheel (Pos 450) engages cannon pinion driving wheel.
14.4
Press cannon pinion (Pos 242) — verify friction
Press cannon pinion onto centre arbor — it must require noticeable resistance. Too easy: tighten with staking set (same stump and punch as for the seconds wheel pipe). Too tight: hands cannot be set, and the mechanism will be damaged. Correct tension: hands can be set by crown, but do not slip freely when crown is released.
Friction fit — must not be loose or excessively tight
14.5
Fit hour wheel (Pos 255/1)
Lower hour wheel assembly over the cannon pinion pipe. Apply a micro-drop of 9010 to the hour wheel bore as it seats.
LUBE: 9010 — hour wheel bore
14.6
Fit dial — tighten dial fasteners (Pos 144) gently
Lower dial onto dial supports (Pos 145). Tighten the two set screws (Pos 144) — snug only. These screws are delicate and the dial feet are fragile. The dial must sit flat with no rocking before the fasteners are tightened.
Dial fastener screws — snug only, do not overtighten

Step 17 · Final Quality Control

Final QC Checks

Do not case the movement until every check passes. Document results.

17.1
Amplitude — 270–310° dial up, fully wound
Check on timing machine. Below 250° in any position: investigate before casing. Note positional amplitude drop — dial up to dial down should not drop more than 25–30°.
17.2
Beat error ≤ 0.2 ms
Adjust using the ETACHRON regulator corrector (Pos 358) without removing the balance. Move the corrector in small increments and recheck. The 2824-2 ETACHRON is a two-piece system — both regulator pins pivot together.
17.3
Rate — ±10 seconds/day across all positions
Check dial up, crown up, crown left, crown down, crown down, dial down. Large positional variance (rate changing significantly between positions) indicates hairspring damage, out-of-poise balance, or worn escapement geometry. Non-COSC movement — aim for ±10 s/d as a minimum competent service result.
17.4
Power reserve — approximately 38 hours
Fully wind, run down, record actual figure. Under 32 hours suggests: mainspring not fully wound before test, new mainspring not yet run-in, or excessive barrel friction.
17.5
Inspect all oil rings under 10× magnification
Verify a complete, clean oil ring (meniscus) is visible at every jewel. An empty jewel hole means the oil was not applied — re-oil if accessible. An oil blob that has spread beyond the jewel edge means too much was applied — clean and re-oil.
10× minimum magnification required
17.6
Hand clearance — rotate through full 12 hours
Fit all hands. Rotate slowly through all 12 hours. No hand should contact any other hand, the dial surface, or the crystal at any position. Pay special attention at 12:00 when all hands are stacked, and at the seconds hand tip passing the minute markers.
17.7
Date change timing — within 30 seconds of 12
With hour hand centred precisely on 12, the date change (disc snap) should occur with the minute hand within 30 seconds of 12. If slow, check cannon pinion friction (Pos 242) and motion work mesh (Pos 260 minute wheel).
17.8
Winding and setting functions
Wind 10 full rotations — no slipping. Pull to setting — hands set without resistance. Quick-set date operates without jumping or skipping teeth.
17.9
Rotor — bidirectional winding confirmed
Rotate rotor CW and CCW. Both directions wind the mainspring (ratchet wheel advances one direction only). Any slipping in one direction = failed reversing wheel.
17.10
Case the movement — fit new gaskets — water resistance test
Fit new case gaskets (crown gasket, case back gasket). Re-case. Perform water resistance test per ISO 22810 — wet overpressure test followed by condensation test. Document the test result and the pressure tested to. Sign and date your service record.
Service complete. A well-serviced ETA 2824-2 should perform reliably for 5–7 years before the next overhaul. Document: pre-service timing figures, post-service timing figures, lubricants used, all replaced parts with part numbers, and water resistance test result.
